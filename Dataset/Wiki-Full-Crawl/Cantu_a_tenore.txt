The cantu a tenre (also known in Sardinian language as su tenre, su cuncrdu, su cuntrttu, su cussrtu, s'agorropamntu, su cantu a pra) or canto a tenore in Italian is a style of polyphonic folk singing characteristic of the island of Sardinia (Italy), particularly the region of Barbagia, though some other Sardinian sub-regions bear examples of such tradition.
In 2005, UNESCO proclaimed the cantu a tenore to be an example of intangible cultural heritage.
