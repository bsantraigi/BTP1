Stepan Prokopovych Timoshenko (Ukrainian:   , Russian:   , IPA: [stpan prkofvt tmnk] ()) (December 23, 1878  May 29, 1972), was a Ukrainian and, later, an American engineer and academician. He is considered to be the father of modern engineering mechanics. A founding member of the Ukrainian Academy of Sciences, Timoshenko wrote seminal works in the areas of engineering mechanics, elasticity and strength of materials, many of which are still widely used today. Having started his scientific career in the Russian Empire, Timoshenko emigrated to Yugoslavia during the Russian Civil War and then to the United States.
