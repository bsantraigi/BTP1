Gustavus Woodson Smith (November 30, 1821  June 24, 1896), more commonly known as G.W. Smith, was a career United States Army officer who fought in the MexicanAmerican War, a civil engineer, and a major general in the Confederate States Army during the American Civil War. He briefly commanded the Army of Northern Virginia from May 31 until June 1, 1862, following the wounding of General Joseph E. Johnston at the Battle of Seven Pines, and before General Robert E. Lee took command. Smith later served as Interim Confederate Secretary of War and in the Georgia state militia.
