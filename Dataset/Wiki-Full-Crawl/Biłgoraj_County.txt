Bigoraj County (Polish: powiat bigorajski) is a unit of territorial administration and local government (powiat) in Lublin Voivodeship, eastern Poland. It was established on January 1, 1999, as a result of the Polish local government reforms passed in 1998. Its administrative seat and largest town is Bigoraj, which lies 79 kilometres (49 mi) south of the regional capital Lublin. The county contains three other towns: Tarnogrd, lying 21 km (13 mi) south of Bigoraj, Jzefw, lying 24 km (15 mi) east of Bigoraj, and Frampol, 16 km (10 mi) north of Bigoraj.
The county covers an area of 1,677.79 square kilometres (647.8 sq mi). As of 2008, its total population is 103,661, which includes 27,225 in Bigoraj, 3,372 in Tarnogrd, 2,450 in Jzefw, 1,415 in Frampol, and a rural population of 69,805.
