The Queen of Navarre (Italian:La regina di Navarra) is a 1942 Italian historical film directed by Carmine Gallone and starring Elsa Merlini, Gino Cervi and Renato Cialente. It was made at the Cinecitt Studios in Rome, based on a play by Eugne Scribe. The film portrays a series of intrigues at the Madrid court of Charles V in the Sixteenth century.
