Benavente is a town and municipality in the north of the province of Zamora, in the autonomous community Castile and Len of Spain. It has about 20,000 inhabitants.
Located north of the capital on an important communications hub, it was repopulated by King Ferdinand II of Len, who also awarded it law-codes (a fuero) in 1167. It was originally known as Malgrat or Malgrado.
