The Nine Lives of Fritz the Cat is a 1974 American adult animated comedy film directed by Robert Taylor and written by Taylor. It features a series of drug-induced vignettes both related and unrelated to life in the 1970s.
Skip Hinnant reprises his role as the eponymous feline protagonist. The film is a sequel to Fritz the Cat, the first animated film to receive an X rating in the United States. Unlike its predecessor, The Nine Lives of Fritz the Cat received an R rating, the first cartoon feature film to do so.
The film was entered into the 1974 Cannes Film Festival but was a commercial failure. The film received mixed reviews from critics.
