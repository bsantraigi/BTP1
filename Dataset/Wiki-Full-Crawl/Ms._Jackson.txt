"Ms. Jackson" is a song by American alternative hip hop duo OutKast. It was released on October 24, 2000, as the second single from their fourth album, Stankonia. It topped the US charts, It also reached number one in Germany and number two in the United Kingdom. In October 2011, NME placed it at number 81 on its list of the "150 Best Tracks of the Past 15 Years" and in June of the same year Rolling Stone ranked it at number 55 in their "100 Best Songs of the 2000s". and won a BET Award for Video of the Year, Grammy Award for Best Rap Performance by a Duo or Group and MTV Video Music Award for Best Hip-Hop Video.
The single helped sell the album following the commercial underperformance of the lead single "B.O.B.", which, despite nearly universal acclaim from critics, failed to chart on the US Billboard Hot 100 and only reached number 69 on the Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart.
