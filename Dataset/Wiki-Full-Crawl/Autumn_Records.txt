Autumn Records was a 1960s San Francisco-based pop record label.  Among the notable acts on its roster was The Beau Brummels, a band who released a pair of top 20 singles, "Laugh, Laugh" and "Just a Little".Also on the Autumn Records roster was The Great Society, a short-lived Haight-Ashbury group that recorded the first version of "Somebody to Love" on Autumn's short-lived North Beach label, which became a 1967 hit for Jefferson Airplane.The label dissolved in 1966. Tom Donahue, a San Francisco DJ who worked for KSAN radio, owned the record label. Donahue invented the genre "underground radio."
