The 1984 German Grand Prix was a Formula One motor race held at Hockenheimring on 5 August 1984. It was race 11 of 16 in the 1984 FIA Formula One World Championship.
The 44-lap race was won by Alain Prost, driving a McLaren-TAG, who also took pole position and set the fastest lap. Teammate Niki Lauda finished second, completing McLaren's second 1-2 finish of the season, while Derek Warwick was third in a Renault, which would turn out to be the final podium finish of his career. Nigel Mansell (Lotus-Renault), Patrick Tambay (Renault) and Ren Arnoux (Ferrari) rounded out the top six.
