Lbork County (Polish: powiat lborski) is a unit of territorial administration and local government (powiat) in Pomeranian Voivodeship, northern Poland, on the Baltic coast. It came into being on January 1, 1999, as a result of the Polish local government reforms passed in 1998. Its administrative seat and largest town is Lbork, which lies 61 kilometres (38 mi) west of the regional capital Gdask. The only other town in the county is eba, lying 29 km (18 mi) north-west of Lbork.
The county covers an area of 706.99 square kilometres (273.0 sq mi). As of 2006 its total population is 63,659, out of which the population of Lbork is 35,069, that of eba is 3,824, and the rural population is 24,766.

Lbork County is bordered by Wejherowo County to the east, Kartuzy County to the south-east, Bytw County to the south and Supsk County to the west. It also borders the Baltic Sea to the north.
